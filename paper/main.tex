\begin{center}
{\LARGE\bfseries\color{geoblue}
GeoDiff-GAN: Evidence-Constrained Prompt-Guided\\
Diffusion-GAN for Sentinel-2 Super-Resolution\par}
\vspace{4pt}
{\large Anonymous Author(s)}\\
{\small Research implementation draft, June 2026}
\end{center}

\begin{abstract}
Fourfold satellite image super-resolution is fundamentally ill posed: many native-resolution
scenes can produce nearly identical low-resolution observations after optical blur, sampling,
noise, and quantization. Deterministic models often suppress uncertain high-frequency detail,
whereas adversarial and diffusion models can produce perceptually plausible but spatially
unsupported structure. We propose \emph{GeoDiff-GAN}, a prompt-optional architecture for synthetic
\SI{40}{m}-to-\SI{10}{m} Sentinel-2 L2A RGB super-resolution. The model decomposes reconstruction
into a deterministic SwinIR-style base and a stochastic residual path. A degradation-conditioned
latent diffusion model samples a compact residual representation; a GeoMapper converts it into
spatial content, four layer-wise FiLM controls, an LR-supported evidence-confidence map, and a
separate prompt-conditioned edit-permission map. A dual-head adversarial decoder predicts
high-frequency reconstruction detail independently from full-band synthetic edits. In SR mode,
detail is confidence-gated and the output undergoes iterative sensor back-projection. Across
stochastic samples, disagreement triggers abstention toward the deterministic base. Edit mode
activates only the permission-gated edit head and labels its output as synthetic. This first part of the
manuscript defines the data model, architecture, training curriculum, and pre-registered
evaluation. Quantitative claims are deferred until geographically separated tile-level
experiments and ablations are complete.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Sentinel-2 provides multispectral Earth observation with \SI{10}{m} sampling for visible RGB
bands and systematic global coverage \citep{drusch2012sentinel}. Increased apparent spatial
resolution can support visualization and downstream analysis, but a fourfold inverse problem
cannot uniquely recover unobserved sub-pixel structure. Existing Sentinel-2 super-resolution
methods demonstrate that learned priors can improve reconstruction under controlled degradations
\citep{lanaras2018super}; however, fidelity and perceptual realism remain competing objectives.

GeoDiff-GAN addresses this tension by separating \emph{stable evidence} from \emph{uncertain
detail}. The deterministic branch estimates geometry and broad reflectance, while diffusion and
adversarial learning operate on a residual whose influence is constrained by LR features, a
spatial gate, high-pass filtering, and re-degradation consistency.

\textbf{Proposed contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item A deterministic--stochastic residual decomposition for satellite SR.
  \item A dual-policy GeoMapper that separates reconstruction confidence from edit authority.
  \item A dual-head residual decoder separating high-pass evidence detail from full-band edits.
  \item Confidence calibration and stochastic uncertainty abstention toward the deterministic base.
  \item Dual spatial and Haar-frequency adversarial supervision used only during training.
  \item A tile-isolated evaluation protocol combining fidelity, perception, consistency,
        uncertainty, and prompt behavior.
\end{itemize}

Evidence confidence controls reconstruction detail, edit permission controls a separate synthetic
residual head, and stochastic disagreement drives abstention toward the deterministic base.}

\section{Background and Problem Formulation}
\subsection{Super-resolution priors}
Transformer restoration networks such as SwinIR exploit shifted-window attention to obtain strong
deterministic reconstructions \citep{liang2021swinir}. Perceptual GAN methods such as ESRGAN
encourage natural high-frequency statistics \citep{wang2018esrgan}, but adversarial objectives do
not by themselves guarantee agreement with the observed LR image. Diffusion models provide a
tractable stochastic prior \citep{ho2020ddpm}; SR3 applies iterative refinement to conditional
super-resolution \citep{saharia2021sr3}, while latent diffusion reduces computation by denoising a
compressed representation \citep{rombach2022ldm}. GeoDiff-GAN assigns these model families
different roles instead of asking one unrestricted generator to solve all objectives.

\subsection{Observation model}
Let $\vect{x}\in[0,1]^{3\times512\times512}$ be a native \SI{10}{m} RGB patch and
$\vect{y}\in[0,1]^{3\times128\times128}$ its synthetic \SI{40}{m} observation:
\begin{equation}
  \vect{y} =
  Q_{\theta_q}\!\left[
    \downarrow_4\!\left(k_{\theta_b} * \vect{x}\right)
    + \vect{n}_{P,\theta_p} + \vect{n}_{G,\theta_g}
  \right]
  = \Dtheta(\vect{x}) + \vect{n}.
  \label{eq:observation}
\end{equation}
Here $k_{\theta_b}$ represents a randomized modulation-transfer/point-spread blur,
$\downarrow_4$ is area downsampling, and $\vect{\theta}$ records blur, Gaussian noise, Poisson
strength, and quantization. The task is to estimate a plausible $\hat{\vect{x}}$ while minimizing
both HR error and the evidence discrepancy
\begin{equation}
 E_{\mathrm{LR}}=\left\|\Dtheta(\hat{\vect{x}})-\vect{y}_{\mathrm{clean}}\right\|_1.
\end{equation}
Because $\Dtheta$ removes high frequencies, low $E_{\mathrm{LR}}$ is necessary but not sufficient:
different HR details may share the same LR projection.

\figblock{infographic_task.png}{0.97}{0.285}{Paired task construction shown through the same
satellite scene: native \SI{10}{m} target, optical blur and sampling, synthetic \SI{40}{m} input,
reconstructed estimate, and the remaining spatial error.}

\textbf{Two output policies.} SR mode is evaluated as reconstruction and must remain strongly
consistent with the input. Edit mode is a prompt-conditioned visualization policy, not an
observation of the ground truth. This distinction is encoded in the network and output metadata.

\section{Data Construction and Training Targets}
\subsection{Sentinel-2 patch extraction}
Complete Sentinel-2 L2A SAFE products are read by raster window rather than loaded as full tiles.
B4, B3, and B2 are scaled by $10{,}000$ to surface-reflectance-like RGB values. Scene
Classification Layer masks reject no-data, defective pixels, cloud shadow, medium/high cloud,
cirrus, snow/ice, non-finite values, zeros, and saturation. Each accepted HR patch is
$512\times512$ with stride 384, corresponding to approximately
\SI{5.12}{km} by \SI{5.12}{km}. The \SI{20}{m} SCL window is obtained by mapping the
\SI{10}{m} RGB bounds into the SCL transform, preventing right/bottom out-of-range reads.

An additional auditable filter detects zero-filled edge and corner background. Rejected files are
moved to quarantine, the original manifest is preserved, and rejection fractions are recorded.
This avoids silently training on padded tile borders while keeping every exclusion reversible.

\subsection{Geographic isolation}
All windows from an MGRS tile receive the same deterministic split. The intended allocation is
approximately 70\% train, 15\% validation, and 15\% test, but percentages are reported at both tile
and patch level. Adjacent or overlapping patches must never cross splits. A research-scale corpus
targets 40,000--100,000 valid patches across urban, agricultural, forest, coast, desert, and
mountain environments; one tile is accepted only for software smoke testing.

\subsection{Residual target and captions}
The base prediction is
\begin{equation}
  \vect{b}=B_{\psi}(\vect{y}), \qquad
  \vect{r}^{*}=\vect{x}-\vect{b}.
\end{equation}
Diffusion learns the distribution of $\vect{r}^{*}$ rather than the complete RGB image. Optional
captions are generated offline from HR patches using Qwen3-VL and stripped of coordinates and
place names. During SR training, a frozen SigLIP text encoder \citep{zhai2023siglip} provides token
context; Qwen is never loaded into the SR optimization graph.

\section{GeoDiff-GAN Architecture}
\subsection{Deterministic base and residual VAE}
The base branch follows a compact SwinIR design \citep{liang2021swinir}: a 60-channel embedding,
six shifted-window residual blocks, two PixelShuffle $\times2$ stages, and a bicubic skip. Its
purpose is conservative radiometric and geometric reconstruction. A VAE
\citep{kingma2014autoencoding} encodes $\vect{r}^{*}$ into a four-channel
$64\times64$ latent:
\begin{equation}
 q_{\phi}(\vect{z}_0|\vect{r}^{*})
 =\mathcal{N}\!\left(\vect{\mu}_{\phi},
 \operatorname{diag}(\exp \vect{\ell}_{\phi})\right).
\end{equation}
This $8\times$ spatial compression makes stochastic denoising substantially cheaper than operating
at $512\times512$ RGB resolution.

\subsection{Conditional latent diffusion}
The LR encoder produces feature grids
$f_{128},f_{64},f_{32},f_{16}$ with channels $64,128,256,256$. The latent and $f_{64}$ share a
$64\times64$ grid. At timestep $t$,
\begin{equation}
 \vect{z}_t=\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}\vect{z}_0+
 \sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}\vect{\epsilon},
 \quad
 \vect{v}_t=\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}\vect{\epsilon}
 -\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}\vect{z}_0 .
\end{equation}
The U-Net predicts $\hat{\vect{v}}$ from $[\vect{z}_t;f_{64}]$, timestep, degradation vector, mode
token, and text context. Channel widths are $[128,256,384,512]$ with cross-attention at the
intermediate scales. Classifier-free guidance can combine conditional and null predictions
\citep{ho2022classifierfree}.

\subsection{GeoMapper and decoder}
GeoMapper receives $[\hat{\vect{z}}_0;f_{64}]$ and returns
\begin{equation}
 (\vect{c},\{\vect{s}_i\}_{i=1}^{4},\vect{c}_e,\vect{c}_p)
 =M_{\omega}(\hat{\vect{z}}_0,f_{64},\vect{e}_{text},m),
\end{equation}
where $\vect{c}\in\mathbb{R}^{128\times64\times64}$ is spatial content,
$\vect{s}_i\in\mathbb{R}^{256}$ are four FiLM controls \citep{perez2018film}, and
$\vect{c}_e,\vect{c}_p\in[0,1]^{1\times64\times64}$ are evidence confidence and edit permission.
The evidence head receives latent content and projected LR evidence but no text context. The edit
head receives content, text context, and confidence, and is forced to zero in SR mode. The decoder
upsamples through 64, 128, 256, and 512 grids with channels $[128,96,64,48]$, fusing LR skips and
stage-specific styles before independent detail and edit $\tanh$ heads.

\section{Evidence Control and Adversarial Learning}
\subsection{Residual output controller}
The decoder is not allowed to replace the complete HR image. In SR mode the detail residual
$\vect{r}_d$ is high-pass filtered and confidence gated:
\begin{equation}
 \vect{r}_{\mathrm{SR}}=
 \left(\vect{r}_d-G_{\sigma}*\vect{r}_d\right)\odot\vect{c}_e,\qquad
 \hat{\vect{x}}_0=\operatorname{clip}(\vect{b}+\vect{r}_{\mathrm{SR}},0,1).
\end{equation}
Three approximate back-projection steps then reduce clean LR discrepancy:
\begin{equation}
 \hat{\vect{x}}_{k+1}=
 \operatorname{clip}\!\left[
 \hat{\vect{x}}_{k}
 \eta\,\uparrow_4
 \left(\vect{y}_{\mathrm{clean}}-\Dtheta(\hat{\vect{x}}_k)\right)
 \right],
 \quad \eta=0.5.
\end{equation}
This is a soft evidence constraint, not a proof of unique recovery. In edit mode,
\begin{equation}
 \vect{r}_{\mathrm{edit}}=
 \left(\vect{r}_d-G_{\sigma}*\vect{r}_d\right)\odot\vect{c}_e+
 \vect{r}_e\odot\vect{c}_p.
\end{equation}
One smaller update uses $\eta=0.15$, and
\texttt{synthetic\_edit=true} is written to metadata.

\subsection{Confidence calibration and abstention}
For paired synthetic training, evidence confidence targets an exponential transform of smoothed
local ungated reconstruction error. At evaluation, stochastic pixel variance $u$ yields agreement
$a=\exp(-u/s_u)$ and combined confidence $\vect{c}_e a$. Because each sample is already
evidence-gated, only the additional agreement factor blends the stochastic mean toward the base:
\begin{equation}
 \hat{\vect{x}}_{\mathrm{abstain}}=
 \vect{b}+a(\hat{\vect{x}}_{\mathrm{mean}}-\vect{b}).
\end{equation}

\subsection{Dual conditional discriminators}
Conditional PatchGANs \citep{isola2017image} receive an HR candidate concatenated with resized LR
evidence at three scales. A second discriminator operates on concatenated Haar LH, HL, and HH
bands plus an LR condition. Spectral-normalized convolutions produce local logits. With real
scores $D(x)$ and fake scores $D(\hat{x})$,
\begin{align}
 L_D &= \mathbb{E}[\max(0,1-D(x))]
      +\mathbb{E}[\max(0,1+D(\hat{x}))],\\
 L_{\mathrm{adv}} &= -\mathbb{E}[D(\hat{x})].
\end{align}
The adversarial generator weight begins at 0.01 so that realism cannot dominate reconstruction and
sensor consistency.

\figblock{infographic_discriminators.png}{0.96}{0.23}{The training-only critics inspect different
properties. Multi-scale lenses compare layout and geometry, while a wavelet prism exposes
directional high-frequency layers for edge and texture inspection.}

\figblock{infographic_modes.png}{0.95}{0.25}{The same LR observation supports two policies. The

\textbf{Spatial-conservation mechanisms.} The model preserves evidence through the deterministic
base, aligned LR features, residual prediction, high-pass control, degradation conditioning,
back-projection, and conditional discriminators. Nevertheless, the inverse remains non-unique,
learned skips may be underused, and the synthetic degradation may differ from a real sensor
pipeline. Claims are therefore restricted to controlled synthetic \SI{40}{m}-to-\SI{10}{m}
experiments.

\section{Five-Stage Optimization}
End-to-end random initialization would create moving interfaces: the base changes the residual
target, the VAE changes the latent distribution, the decoder changes the image-space meaning of
latent error, and discriminators immediately penalize untrained outputs. Training is therefore
staged.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Base:} train SwinIR using Charbonnier, SSIM \citep{wang2004ssim}, gradient, and
        degradation-consistency losses.
  \item \textbf{Residual representation:} train the VAE, LR encoder, GeoMapper, and decoder without
        adversarial loss; monitor reconstruction, KL, wavelet error, and latent statistics.
  \item \textbf{Diffusion:} freeze the decoder path and train SNR-weighted velocity prediction:
        \begin{equation}
          L_{\mathrm{diff}}=
          \mathbb{E}_{t}\!\left[
          w(t)\|\hat{\vect{v}}-\vect{v}_t\|_2^2\right].
        \end{equation}
  \item \textbf{Joint refinement:} train diffusion, LR encoder, GeoMapper, decoder, and separate
        discriminators with reconstruction, perceptual, frequency, consistency, and low-weight
        GAN objectives.
  \item \textbf{Edit tuning:} adapt diffusion/mapper/decoder using prompt alignment, permission
        supervision, localization, and softer LR consistency. Paired-HR reconstruction losses are
        omitted for counterfactual prompts.
\end{enumerate}

The initial joint generator objective is
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
 L_G={}&1.0L_{\mathrm{char}}+1.0L_{\mathrm{cons}}
 +0.2L_{\mathrm{SSIM}}+0.1L_{\mathrm{LPIPS}}\\
 &+0.05L_{\mathrm{wavelet}}+0.1L_{\mathrm{cal}}
 +0.05L_{\mathrm{prompt}}
 +0.01L_{\mathrm{adv}},
\end{split}
\end{equation}
where LPIPS is a learned perceptual distance \citep{zhang2018unreasonable}. Mixed precision,
gradient checkpointing, batch size 1--2 per GPU, accumulation to effective batch 16, and DDP are
used for the full model.

\subsection{Prompt and mode conditioning}
Offline captions describe observable land cover, objects, density, terrain, and texture. Forty
percent of batches use a null prompt to retain unconditional SR competence; paraphrases reduce
surface-form dependence; mismatches test whether spatial evidence can override incompatible text.
Batch-size-one training draws mismatches from a coordinate-free counterfactual prompt pool. The
mode token selects the output policy. Null, matched, and paraphrased prompts suppress edit
permission; mismatches target sparse permission coverage. This mechanism is a research hypothesis:
confidence calibration and permission localization must be measured before claiming reduced
hallucination.

\section{Experimental Protocol and Falsifiable Novelty}
The proposed novelty is the \emph{specific evidence-controlled integration}, not the independent
existence of SwinIR, VAEs, diffusion, FiLM, GANs, wavelets, or back-projection. Each claim is tied
to a controlled ablation:
\begin{itemize}
  \item deterministic base versus unrestricted full-image generation;
  \item dual-policy GeoMapper versus one shared gate, no confidence calibration, and no permission
        supervision;
  \item dual residual heads versus one shared residual and an edit head exposed in SR mode;
  \item confidence-only, uncertainty-only, combined abstention, and variance visualization only;
  \item spatial GAN, wavelet GAN, both, and no adversarial supervision;
  \item fixed degradation, randomized unconditioned degradation, conditioned degradation, and
        conditioned degradation with back-projection;
  \item shared versus mode-specific residual/projection policies.
\end{itemize}

Baselines are bicubic, SwinIR, ESRGAN, diffusion-only, and GAN-only residual models. All methods
must use identical tile splits, RGB scaling, masks, degradation draws, metric implementations, and
documented compute budgets. Eight stochastic samples per test patch yield a mean prediction and
pixelwise variance. Metrics include PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, DISTS
\citep{ding2020dists}, tolerance-aware edge F1, and clean LR re-degradation error. Edit mode is
evaluated with image--text alignment, realism, edit magnitude, localization, and LR consistency,
not target PSNR for counterfactual prompts.

Patches within a tile are correlated; therefore, the statistical unit for geographic claims is the
held-out tile. Paired method differences, dispersion, confidence intervals, and the number of
tiles will be reported. Separate analyses cover unseen cities, unseen MGRS tiles, land-cover
groups, degradation severity, uncertainty calibration, and failure cases.
